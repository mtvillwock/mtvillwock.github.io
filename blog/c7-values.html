Affirming Values against Stereotype Threat

Published Nov. 23rd, 2014

Evaluating my values

During this week of Phase 0, I was asked to reflect on some values that come to mind when I think of "happiest, the proudest, or the most satisfied" moments and then talk about why one of the values mattered to me. I came up with this list:

  • Challenge
  • Community
  • Growth
  • Friendships
  • Loyalty
  • Movement
  • Self-Knowledge

I place an importance on Community because I believe that who you associate with impacts you nearly as much as anything that you do individually. There are studies and articles about this phenomenon.

I generally try to live up to these values, but as you can see from my rankings, I'm not totally where I'd like to be with all of them.

Disagree 1 ----- 2 ----- 3 ----- 4 ----- 5 Agree

  • Challenge 4
  • Community 3
  • Growth 4
  • Friendships 4
  • Loyalty 5
  • Movement 2
  • Self-Knowledge 2

The reason for the neutral score in Community is because I am not as aggressive about seeking out new people to associate with or befriend and I also am not great at disassociating myself from people who do not benefit me. Loyalty is a factor in that.

The low score in movement because I haven't traveled much (geographically and, dare I say it, academically/intellectually) and thus I have a limited perception of the world. I am working to fix this. The low score in self-knowledge is because I don't feel like I've had enough experiences to know "who I am" yet.

The last topic I gave advice on was actually blog writing for a friend, which you can read here.

Weighing my values against the stereotype threat

I feel like there is room for improvement in my values. I think my values are good and important but not optimal yet. I think that I can mediate stereotype threat when I feel it (I believe I was doing that earlier this week, actually) by affirming my values of growth and challenge, as well as relying on my community to affirm that my abilities "are assumed, not questioned" at DBC.

I think there are many things that could help me when I feel down at DBC. Taking a walk, looking at old challenges that I vanquished, talking with a classmate, speaking with the counselor, taking deep breaths, getting some food, and more could all help me feel better.

That's all I got this week. Hopefully this told you a bit about my values. If you're interested in more about affirmation and stereotype threat, read this article. Thanks for reading!
